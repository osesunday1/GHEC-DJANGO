SCHOOLING IN CANADA

About Schools in Canada

Canada is renowned for its high-quality post-secondary education system, offering a wide range of options for students seeking undergraduate and graduate studies. Universities offer a diverse range of programs, including bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees, while colleges primarily focus on diploma and certificate programs. Both universities and colleges in Canada are recognized for their academic excellence and research contributions. Many Canadian universities and colleges provide co-op and internship programs, integrating practical work experience with academic studies. These programs offer students the opportunity to gain real-world skills, apply their knowledge in professional settings, d establish valuable connections within their chosen industries.

Work After Schooling

The Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) is designed for international students who have completed a program of study at a designated Canadian educational institution. The PGWP allows eligible graduates to work in Canada for a period ranging from 8 months to 3 years, depending on the length of their program. The work experience gained through the PGWP can be valuable for enhancing career prospects and potentially transitioning to permanent residency.

Budget

Canadian universities have different tuition fee structures for international students. The fees can vary depending on the province, institution, and program. On average, international undergraduate students can expect to pay between 15,000 to 40,000 CAD per year.


Processing fee: N250,000

CANADA EXPRESS ENTRY

Express Entry

Canada Express Entry is an immigration system introduced by the Canadian government in 2015 to manage the selection and processing of skilled foreign workers who wish to migrate to Canada permanently. It is designed to attract highly skilled individuals who can contribute to the Canadian economy and fill labor market gaps.

Budget

Budgeting for Canada Express Entry involves considering various factors and expenses such as Family Size, Proof of Funds, Language Test, Educational Credential Assessment, etc. A minimum of 14,000 CAD upward should be budgeted.


Processing fee: N500,000

TOURISM

Tourism Visa

If you are planning to visit Canada for tourism purposes, you may need to apply for a visitor visa, also known as a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV)..
To apply for a visitor visa, you will need to complete the appropriate application form, which can be obtained from the official website of Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). The form should be filled out accurately and completely.


Processing fee: N150,000

Business

Canada offers several options for individuals who want to visit the country for business purposes. The specific type of business visa you may need will depend on the nature and duration of your intended business activities. Some common options are: Business Visitor, Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA), International Mobility Program (IMP) etc.
